Biography

I am a PhD Candidate in Economics at Universidad Icesi (Cali, Colombia) and hold an MSc in Economics from the same institution. My research sits at the intersection of labor economics, intergenerational social mobility, and the economics of science, technology, and innovation (STI) systems.

My work combines quantitative econometric methods — including descriptive statistics, mean-difference analyses, count models, and synthetic indicator construction — with primary survey data and longitudinal datasets. Empirically, I have investigated intergenerational income mobility and internal migration in Colombia, agroecological practice adoption among smallholder cacao farmers in Peru, and the measurement of human capital gaps and technological capabilities within Valle del Cauca's regional STI system.

I am co-author of a peer-reviewed article in the Journal of Family Business Management and a contributor to three books published by Editorial Universidad del Valle. My manuscript on intergenerational income mobility and internal migration in Colombia is currently under second-round peer review at the Review of Development Economics.

I have held research positions at Dalhousie University (Halifax, Canada) as an ELAP Fellow and at CIAT–CGIAR (Ucayali, Peru). I currently serve as Lecturer at Universidad Icesi, teaching Economic Policy in English and Principles of Economics in Spanish.
