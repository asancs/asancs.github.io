Biography

I am a PhD Candidate in Economic Sciences at Universidad Icesi (Cali, Colombia) and hold an MSc in Economics from the same institution. My research is grounded in applied microeconomics and focuses on labor markets, poverty and inequality, intergenerational mobility, and public policy evaluation.

I work primarily with quantitative econometric methods — including regression analysis, structural equation models, income transmission models, and count models — applied to large-scale household surveys and longitudinal datasets. My empirical work draws on sources such as the ELCA, GEIH, ECV, GEM, and historical ENH data, as well as primary survey instruments I have helped design and implement.

My doctoral dissertation examines the dynamics of poverty and inequality in Colombia, with attention to the roles of labor markets, migration, and education in shaping social mobility across generations. A related manuscript — Looking for Opportunities: Intergenerational Income Gains and Internal Migration in Colombia — is currently under second-round peer review at the Review of Development Economics.

I am co-author of a peer-reviewed article in the Journal of Family Business Management and have provided technical and research support for three editorial projects published by the Programa Editorial Universidad del Valle. I have held research positions at Dalhousie University (Halifax, Canada) as an ELAP Fellow and at CIAT–CGIAR (Palmira, Colombia). I currently teach at Universidad Icesi in both English and Spanish.
