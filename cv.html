Professional Profile

PhD Candidate in Economic Sciences at Universidad Icesi, specializing in applied microeconomics, labor economics, poverty and inequality, and public policy evaluation. Research is grounded in quantitative econometric methods — including regression analysis, structural equation models, income transmission models, and count models — applied to large-scale household surveys (GEIH, ECV, ELCA, GEM, Refinitiv) and longitudinal datasets. Doctoral dissertation investigates the dynamics of poverty and inequality in Colombia, with attention to the roles of labor markets, migration, and education in intergenerational mobility. Co-author of a peer-reviewed article in the Journal of Family Business Management; manuscript under second-round review at Review of Development Economics. Lecturer at Universidad Icesi in English and Spanish.
