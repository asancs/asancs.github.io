Overview

Current Research Agenda

My research asks how economic opportunities are transmitted — or blocked — across generations, regions, and institutional contexts. I am particularly interested in how internal migration shapes intergenerational income trajectories, how smallholder farmers navigate decisions about agroecological practices, and how regional science, technology, and innovation systems produce or perpetuate structural inequalities in human capital formation.

Methodologically, I work with longitudinal survey data and historical household surveys, applying econometric tools that range from income transmission models to count models for adoption behavior. I also design and implement primary survey instruments for projects where secondary data are insufficient.

Projects

Selected Research Projects

Dissertation · In Progress

The Dynamics of Social Mobility in Colombia

Doctoral dissertation examining the structural determinants of intergenerational income mobility in Colombia, with emphasis on ethnic heterogeneity and short-term longitudinal dynamics. Advisor: Blanca Zuluaga, PhD (Universidad Icesi).

Dalhousie University · 2023–2024

Intergenerational Income Gains and Internal Migration in Colombia

Empirical investigation using the Encuesta Longitudinal Colombiana (ELCA) and historical Encuestas Nacionales de Hogares (ENH: 1976, 1980, 1985, 1991) to estimate whether rural-to-urban migration functions as a mechanism for intergenerational income gains among low-income Colombian households. Conducted during ELAP research residency at Dalhousie University under the supervision of Lars Osberg, PhD.

CIAT–CGIAR · 2024

Agroecological Practices Among Cacao Producers in Ucayali, Peru

Analysis of primary survey data from the Agroecological Practices Characterization Survey (CGIAR Initiative on Agroecology), covering 654 smallholder cacao farmers in Ucayali. Applied mean-difference analyses and count models to assess adoption rates, production efficiency, and barriers to agroecological transition. Supervisors: Genowefa Blundo Canto, PhD & Guillermo Orjuela-Ramirez (CGIAR).

Un Valle del Conocimiento · 2020–2021

Human Capital & Technological Capabilities in Valle del Cauca

Multi-year research program measuring human capital gaps, labor market imbalances, and technological capabilities in Valle del Cauca's C&CTI system. Designed survey instruments, constructed synthetic distance indicators (DP2 method), and developed multi-dimensional dashboards integrating primary and administrative data across 1,233+ participants and companies in Cali, Buenaventura, and Tulua.

Working Paper · In Progress

Short-Term Income Mobility and Ethnic Gaps in Colombia

Longitudinal analysis investigating short-term income mobility trajectories and their relationship with ethnic group membership in Colombia, contributing empirical evidence to debates on structural inequality and mobility bottlenecks.
