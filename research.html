Research Agenda

My research is rooted in applied microeconomics and centers on understanding how poverty and inequality are reproduced — or overcome — across generations. I examine the structural mechanisms through which labor markets, internal migration, and educational attainment shape intergenerational mobility and social class dynamics in Colombia and Latin America.

A unifying thread across my projects is the gap between economic opportunity and its realization: who moves up, under what conditions, and through which pathways. My doctoral dissertation investigates these dynamics in depth, drawing on longitudinal and cross-sectional household data to trace the relationship between poverty, labor market conditions, and intergenerational income transmission.

Beyond mobility, I have contributed empirical work on innovation systems and human capital formation at the regional level, and applied quantitative methods to field survey data in agricultural development contexts. These experiences reinforce my commitment to research that connects economic theory with real-world evidence and informs policy decisions.

Projects

Selected Research Projects

Doctoral Dissertation · In Progress · Universidad Icesi

The Dynamics of Poverty and Inequality in Colombia

Doctoral dissertation examining the determinants of intergenerational mobility in Colombia, with particular attention to the roles of labor markets, migration, and education. Draws on longitudinal household survey data to trace income transmission across generations and identify structural barriers to social mobility. Advisor: Blanca Zuluaga, PhD.

Dalhousie University · 2023–2024 · ELAP Fellow

Intergenerational Income Gains and Internal Migration in Colombia

Empirical analysis of intergenerational income mobility in Colombia, focusing on the timing of migration—whether occurring at birth or during adolescence—as a key mechanism shaping income trajectories among low-income households. The study uses data from the Encuesta Longitudinal Colombiana (ELCA) and historical Encuestas Nacionales de Hogares (ENH: 1976, 1980, 1985, 1991), applying econometric methods in Stata. Conducted under the supervision of Blanca Zuluaga, PhD (Universidad Icesi), with valuable feedback and support from Lars Osberg, PhD (Dalhousie University), particularly during a visiting research stay.

Universidad Icesi · Ongoing

Applied Microeconomics & Finance — Research Collaboration

Ongoing collaboration with research teams at Universidad Icesi on projects in applied microeconomics and finance. Contributions include structural equation modeling, web scraping, database cleaning, quantitative analysis, and academic writing support.

CIAT–CGIAR · 2024 · Palmira, Colombia

Quantitative Analysis of Agroecological Practices — Applied Research Support

Applied quantitative methods to primary field survey data as part of the CGIAR Initiative on Agroecology. Analyzed data from smallholder cacao producers using mean-difference analyses and count models to assess practice adoption and productive efficiency. Contribution as visiting researcher.

Un Valle del Conocimiento · 2020–2021 · Cali, Colombia

Human Capital & Regional Innovation Systems in Valle del Cauca

Multi-year program measuring human capital gaps, labor market imbalances, and technological capabilities within Valle del Cauca's C&CTI system. Designed survey instruments, constructed synthetic distance indicators (DP2 method), and developed multi-dimensional dashboards integrating primary and administrative data. Provided technical and research support for three editorial publications.
